I am Fan Yang, a digital creator who deconstructs visual aesthetics through design thinking. As a UI/UX designer and commercial photographer with six years of experience, I am constantly exploring the golden ratio of human-computer interactionâ€”crafting pixel-perfect interface prototypes in Figma during the day, while capturing the delicate interplay of light and shadow in the real world with my Hasselblad camera at night. My obsession with the underlying logic of products has shaped a unique working methodology. Every week, I meticulously dissect 2-3 popular apps, building an analytical matrix that includes interaction paths, color systems, and motion curves. A recent observation at Starbucks was particularly intriguing: over 73% of users repeatedly tap the screen when loading animations exceed 1.5 seconds, which directly inspired the optimization of the preloading algorithm in the photography app I'm developing. The current focus of my efforts, the "LensCraft" project, is the culmination of this cross-disciplinary thinking. We have developed a dynamic beauty engine based on CNN (Convolutional Neural Network), which intelligently adjusts light and shadow structures while preserving skin texture details, surpassing traditional skin-smoothing algorithms. Even more exciting is our proprietary PerfectionRAW format, which uses multi-frame synthesis technology to achieve DSLR-level dynamic range in smartphone photos. Initial internal testing data shows that the iPhone 16 Pro Max can achieve 15 stops of dynamic range, outperforming some professional cameras. Choosing iOS as the initial launch platform was a deliberate decision. Apple users' acute sensitivity to image quality (with an average of 2.3 professional photography apps installed per user) and the unified hardware standards allow our algorithms to perform at their peak. Once a comprehensive user profile is established, the Android version will adopt a modular architecture, designed to be compatible with over 20 mainstream models.
